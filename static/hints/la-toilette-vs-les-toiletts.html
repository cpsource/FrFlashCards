La Toilette vs Les Toilettes

Understanding a very common French “false friend”

1. Two expressions, two meanings

In English, we often say “the toilet” to mean the bathroom. In French, this is usually translated as « les toilettes », which looks plural: literally “the toilets”.

But there is also « la toilette » in the singular, and it does not mean “the toilet” as a fixture or a room.

Key idea: « la toilette » = washing / grooming « les toilettes » = bathroom / restroom

2. La toilette — washing and grooming

Historically, the word toilette comes from toile (cloth) + the diminutive ending -ette, meaning “little cloth”. Over time, faire sa toilette came to mean doing one’s washing and getting dressed.

• Elle fait sa toilette chaque matin. She washes up / gets ready every morning.
• La toilette du matin prend vingt minutes. The morning wash-up / grooming takes twenty minutes.

So when you see « la toilette » in the singular, think of personal care or grooming, not the bathroom.

3. Les toilettes — the bathroom / restroom

In modern French, when you want to ask where the bathroom is, you say:

• Où sont les toilettes, s’il vous plaît ? Where is the bathroom, please?

Why is it plural? Historically, « les toilettes » referred to all the objects used for grooming: towels, basins, combs, perfumes, etc. There were many items, so the plural form was natural.

Later, as indoor plumbing and bathrooms appeared, the word shifted to mean the room where you go — but the plural form stayed. That’s why today you say:

• Je vais aux toilettes. I’m going to the bathroom.

Note the preposition: « aux toilettes » (to the toilets), not « à la toilette ».

4. Quick comparison

Expression Literal meaning Real meaning in French Typical English equivalent
la toilette the toilet (singular) washing, grooming, getting ready wash-up, grooming routine
faire sa toilette to do one’s toilet to wash and get dressed to get ready / wash up
les toilettes the toilets (plural) bathroom, restroom (the place) the bathroom / the restroom
aller aux toilettes to go to the toilets to go to the bathroom to go to the restroom

5. Mini practice

Decide which one to use: la toilette or les toilettes?

  • a) You want to ask in a café: “Where is the bathroom?” → Où sont ______, s’il vous plaît ?
  • b) You’re describing your morning routine: “She does her wash-up every morning.” → Elle fait sa ______ chaque matin.
  • c) You tell a friend: “Just a second, I’m going to the bathroom.” → Une seconde, je vais aux ______.

Answers:

a) les toilettes — Où sont les toilettes, s’il vous plaît ?
b) la toilette — Elle fait sa toilette chaque matin.
c) les toilettes — Je vais aux toilettes.
